Producer - Degree V Degree
Degree V Degree was a personal project from myself and a fellow student. This project was a livestreamed gameshow, where we took people from
different courses and had them answer various general-knowledge questions. I took up the role as producer on this project with my colleague taking
the role of director. We assembled a team which we both trusted and knew had the abilities required to pull off the project. We had quite limited
resources outside of a studio of which to shoot in. My role mainly comprised of ensureing everyone was pulling thier weight and ensuring we were
on schedule as well as dealing with any possible difficulties we could face along the way. Despite taking more of a backseat in this production I still
helped out where possible to ensure we had the best possible production. In total this was a 8 week project from start to finish. This was broken down
according to plans we had in place to get the correct host, script and best possible contestants for the show itself.

We ended up livestreaming a first pilot episode lasting 25 minutes through both Facebook and across the student campus.
The video of the final project can be seen in the video reel above.
Editor/Cameraman - Kirkleetham Owl Centre
I worked as both an editor and a cameraman for a promotional film for Kirkleethams owl centre. This was an instance where the owl centre
themselves approached our univeristy for some filming experience, I jumped at the oppitunity and got involved as soon as possible.

It was quite a short filming schdule and turn around, with just 2 filming days and a 4 day edit period. Due to the short filming period
I was asked to help with the filming of the various wildlife within the centre which did also include getting into a cage with snow owls,
eagle owls and vultures.

After the filming dates we met up and went through a group edit. I was the one who made all the changes and the suggestions to what we should
change and how we should composite the shot. The video was then diplayed on the Owl centres website. It is no longer on there as they changed it
back in 2018. The video is viewable in the video selection area above this.
